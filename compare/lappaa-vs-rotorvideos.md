## Core Difference: Real-World Footage vs. Graphic Visualizers

**Rotor Videos** is a web-based engine built to solve the "I need a music video" problem. You upload a track, and its AI analyzes the tempo and energy. It then edits together licensed stock footage from its massive library, applying cuts and visual effects that react to the beat. The output feels like a produced music video.

**Lappaa** doesn't use stock footage or attempt to simulate real-world camera cuts. It is an instant generator of graphic **animated visualizers** (spectrums, waveforms, neon rings, CRT lines) mapped mathematically to the audio's frequency. It's built for rapid "audio drops," teasers, and social stories, rather than full-blown cinematic releases.

## Pricing Philosophy

A core differentiator is how the processing is structured.

**Rotor Videos** utilizes a credit system or higher-tier monthly subscriptions. Because it licenses high-end stock footage and processes heavy HD video rendering on its servers, the cost per video is significant unless creating content in bulk.

**Lappaa** leverages the neural engines and GPU already on your iPhone or iPad. Because the processing occurs locally, there are no remote server costs, no expensive stock licensing fees, and therefore no per-video charges or restrictive monthly processing limits.

## Who Should Choose Which?

### Choose **Lappaa** if you

- Are an indie musician, music producer, or DJ dropping daily or weekly snippets to Instagram Reels/TikTok.
- Prefer graphic, abstract frequency visualizers over stock footage.
- Need to normalize your track to a specific LUFS target (e.g. -14 LUFS) to ensure it plays at the right volume on social feeds without blowing out the mix.
- Want a fast mobile workflow without desktop browsers.

### Choose **Rotor Videos** if you

- Are releasing a major single and need a full, 3-minute professional-looking music video driven by real-world footage.
- Have a marketing budget for high-quality stock licensing and server rendering.
- Need highly specific tools like Apple Music Album Motion generation.
